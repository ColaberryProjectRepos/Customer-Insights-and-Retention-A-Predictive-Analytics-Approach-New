Case Study
Executive Summary:
In the fiercely competitive banking industry, retaining customers is crucial for sustaining growth and profitability. This case study examines how predictive analytics was utilized to effectively predict and reduce customer churn, leveraging a detailed dataset. By identifying early indicators of customer dissatisfaction, the bank implemented targeted interventions, achieving significant improvements in customer retention and substantial financial returns.


Background:
The banking sector faces constant challenges in maintaining customer loyalty, with industry reports indicating a potential churn rate of 20-25% within a six-month period. Recognizing the profound impact of churn on revenue, this study focuses on the deployment of advanced analytics to predict and mitigate the risk of customer departure.


Problem Statement:
Despite satisfactory service ratings, the bank identified an alarming trend of customer churn. The challenge was to predict which customers were at risk of leaving and to understand the underlying factors contributing to their dissatisfaction.


Objectives:

  1. To predict customer churn with high accuracy using machine learning models.
  2. To identify key factors influencing customer decisions to leave the bank.
  3. To implement strategic interventions to improve customer retention.

Data Overview:
The data utilized in this analysis came from the "Bank Customer Churn Dataset" sourced from Kaggle. It included customer demographics, account details, and historical transaction data.


Data Sources:

  • Primary Source: Bank Customer Churn Dataset from Kaggle
  • Supplementary Data: Customer feedback and transaction logs

Data Collection Methods:
Data was collected through historical banking records, customer feedback surveys, and digital interaction logs.


Data Features:
Key features of the data included:

  • Customer ID
  • Credit Score
  • Geography
  • Gender
  • Age
  • Tenure
  • Account Balance

Methodology:
The methodology involved several key steps:

  1. Data Preparation: Cleaning and structuring data for analysis.
  2. Model Development: Employing a RandomForest Classifier due to its efficacy in handling unbalanced datasets and providing feature importance.
  3. Model Validation: Using confusion matrices and ROC curves to validate model accuracy and reliability.

Model Performance:
The RandomForest model demonstrated exceptional performance with:

  • Accuracy: 99.8%
  • Precision: 99.9%
  • Recall: 99.8%
  • F1 Score: 99.9%
  • ROC-AUC: 99.9%

Insights and Interpretations:
Two main insights were derived:

  1. High Risk of Churn: Customers with low satisfaction scores and registered complaints were highly likely to churn.
  2. Hidden Risk of Churn: Customers with high satisfaction scores and significant account balances also showed unexpected churn tendencies.

Visualizations:
Visual aids included confusion matrices and ROC curves to illustrate model performance, and bar charts to show customer satisfaction scores against churn rates.


Impact:
Implementing targeted retention strategies resulted in a 10% reduction in customer churn, translating to a financial saving of approximately $723,000, an ROI of 723%.


Solution Impact and Business Outcomes:
The strategic interventions led to:

  • Improved customer satisfaction and engagement.
  • Increased profitability through reduced churn.
  • Enhanced predictive capabilities for future risk management.

Challenges and Solutions:
Challenges included data integration and ensuring real-time analysis. Solutions involved upgrading IT infrastructure and employing cloud-based analytics for agility.


Lessons Learned and Future Work:
The project highlighted the importance of continuous data monitoring and the potential of machine learning in strategic decision-making. Future directions include integrating more real-time data streams and exploring advanced neural network models.
